📻 RTL-SDR Guide — Kids Primary SDR Tool

RTL-SDR is receive-only — it cannot transmit. This makes it 100% safe for kids!

What You Need

  • RTL-SDR USB dongle (RTL2832U + R820T2 chipset)
  • An antenna (usually included with the dongle)
  • A computer with USB port

Frequency Range

RTL-SDR receives from 24 MHz to ~1.7 GHz. This covers FM radio (88-108 MHz), aircraft (1090 MHz), weather satellites (137 MHz), and much more.

Software Options

  • SDR# (Windows) — Most popular for beginners
  • GQRX (Linux/Mac) — Open source, great interface
  • CubicSDR (Cross-platform) — Works everywhere

Fun Things to Receive

  • 📻 FM Radio stations
  • ✈️ Aircraft positions (ADS-B at 1090 MHz)
  • 🛰️ Weather satellite images (NOAA at 137 MHz)
  • 📡 Amateur radio communications
  • 🌡️ Weather station data (433 MHz)
⚠️ Always check your local laws about which frequencies you can legally listen to.
